# Code of Conduct

*markwork* is a technical project, and its shared space is for the
technical work alone. Other topics, however interesting to the people
involved, are those people's own affair. The project disclaims any part
in any matter other than the project itself, and any and all
responsibility for the conduct of any person.

The project takes notice of contributions, not contributors. A patch
is judged on what it does, an issue on what it reports, and nothing
else. Who a person is elsewhere, what they have said elsewhere, or
what others say about them is outside the scope of this project, and
demands that the project act on such things will be declined without
consideration of their merits. The project extends no one credit on
the same grounds.

There shall be no *markwork* Discord. There shall be no *markwork*
subreddit. There shall be no such thing. Communication happens on the
repository's issues and nowhere else, though it may move to another
platform in the future without notice. Keep what you write there about
*markwork*. Whatever the platform, its own terms of service govern it,
and matters belonging to the platform should be reported to the
platform, which employs people for that purpose.

The primary language is English. Allowance is made for those who
command less of it, so read others generously and do not mistake
limited English for a limited point.

There is no project process for reporting other people, and none is
wanted. GitHub lets one account ignore another, and a persistent troll
may simply be ignored in that way. Content that is off topic or is
spam may be removed without comment. That, and the platform's own
remedies, is the whole of the enforcement here.

Verbal harm *is* harm, and the internet enables communication under
conditions such as anonymity that make such harm a real challenge. This
project is based on the hope that positive outcomes are possible as well.
Nevertheless, the reality is that an open-source tracker cannot provide
remedy except by removing content violating the above.

For remedy, there is help available almost everywhere. The people who
provide it chose this work and trained for years to do it well. What
feels unspeakable to you is, to them, familiar ground: they have heard
it before, and they know what to do with it. Theirs is a real
discipline, studied and improved like any other, and contacting them
is known to help.
